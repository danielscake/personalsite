SAP Palo Alto

In the summer of 2016, I was hired by SAP Labs Palo Alto as a Videographer. The team that I worked for is named Design X. Design X created educational content about design thinking and implementing design thinking in large organizations for SAP and for partners externally. As part of this, the team worked with Sam Yen (Chief Design Officer of SAP) and Stanford University.

I was tasked with developing experiences through video. The team wanted students to understand and experience what other students had gone through during and after completing their course (Organizational Psychology of Design Thinking: Empowering Design Minded Intrapreneurs) course. The course was taught at the Hasso Plattner Institute of Design at Stanford by Alex Scully, Laura Pickel, and Sam Yen. During the summer I coordinated, interviewed, recorded, edited and created videos of nineteen prior students and four lecturers. I also completed a video which combined many of these interviews together. The video was then premiered to over 150 executives at SAP to help explain what the Design X team was doing at Stanford and why.

That summer at SAP taught me many important lessons about how to write better emails and how to organize many individuals with complicated schedules. I learned how to interview people and get them to talk. I learned how to apply my prior video skills with people skills and learned a lot about empathy and applying empathy. The workshops that I participated in such as a Design Thinking workshop and the popular Crucial Conversations workshop taught me how to think differently and have conversations that are necessary. And finally, the many Design Thinkers that I was always surrounded by taught me about how to think more efficiently, more empathetically, and completely changed the way that I think about problems.
