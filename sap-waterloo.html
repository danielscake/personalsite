SAP Labs Waterloo

In the summer of 2017, I was hired by SAP Labs Waterloo as a UI/UX Designer. I worked in the marketing department developing different projects to market SAPâ€™s Predictive Analytics Tool. Over the course of the summer, there were two main projects that I worked on. One was a demo called Catch and the other was a website for our partner The Toronto International Film Festival.

I worked on the design, and front-end of both of these projects. Based on the company culture and requirements of these projects I did not get enough time to test as I usually do. I also did not have much of a say in the design thinking aspects of the projects. The Catch project had already been started from the co-ops before me and the TIFF project did not give enough time for design thinking. We needed to get from a non-functioning app to a functioning app in little time. Both projects focused on actually making the app work rather than design and UX.

After spending about a month working on the Catch site and backend, we had to move to working on the TIFF site completely. Because of limitations on the partner side for TIFF that project did not get finished.

I had started the term thinking that I would be designing and building UI/UX interfaces and a front-end dev would build them. This was not the case - it turned out that I was the front-end dev. This meant that I had limited time to design, learn, build, and test my web pages.

It was hard but, I learned a lot about front-end dev during my time at SAP in Waterloo.
